About Me | John DeLoretta

Hello!


You've stumbled upon my page, so let me tell you a little about myself.

I love everything GIS and map related. Joining spatial information with non-spatial, tabular data is one of the most powerful tools in data analysis. They say "seeing is believing," and anyone familiar with GIS knows this to be absolutely true. A map can tell you so much more than charts and graphs and the only way to find these hidden messages is to watch your data come alive through geography. The only thing better than being able to view and interact with your data in this light is the ability to share this insight with others. In my opinion, there's almost nothing on earth that can't be improved through GIS and spatial analysis.

Professional Experience in a Nutshell


I am a professional GIS enthusiast with 11 years' experience in map production, data management, and data analysis as an environmental consultant. I have worked for many types of clients including agencies under the U.S. Department of Defense, federal and state transportation departments, private oil and gas clients, telecommunications, local water/sewer authorities, and private development clients.

Projects I have worked on range from wetland delineations, groundwater sampling, environmental soil sampling, environmental site assessments, cultural resource investigations, water/sewer asset management, pipeline class location determination studies, Naval firing range design, unexploded ordnance investigations, ground penetrating radar studies, to recreational lake capacity/usage studies.
